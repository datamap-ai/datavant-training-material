1.2 Low-Risk Onboarding Form

The Low-Risk Form is exclusively reserved for Tier 4 suppliers, defined as suppliers with no system access and no data access of any kind (No PII, PHI, system or any other data access).

Mandatory requirements to submit the Low-Risk Form:

  • Tax Documentation (see Section 1.4)
  • Banking Details (Bank Letterhead or Voided or Canceled Check, see Section 1.3)
⚠️
Do NOT use the Low-Risk Form to onboard a supplier that does not qualify as Tier 4. Using this form for suppliers with system or data access creates downstream security and compliance risks. When in doubt, follow the standard onboarding process in Section 1.1.

1.3 Banking Details Requirements

Suppliers must provide one of the following for ACH payment setup:

  • Bank Letterhead confirming ACH / banking details, OR
  • Voided or Canceled Check copy

1.4 Mandatory Tax Documentation

System profiles must contain the correct verified filing attachments based on jurisdiction rules:

🇺🇸 US Entity / Individual

Required asset: Form W-9

Confirms formal internal taxpayer registration status and tracking indexes.

🌍 Foreign Individual

Required asset: Form W-8BEN

Certifies foreign individual status and determines applicable US withholding tax when paid by a US entity.

🏢 Foreign Company / Entity

Required asset: Form W-8BEN-E

Certifies global corporate operational status and validates appropriate tax withholdings under a US entity payment track.

📍 Local Cross-Border Orgs

Required asset: Regional Registry File

At least one required document: VAT Cert, COI, GSTIN, Proof of Business Address, or UTR. VAT or COI is strongly preferred.

⚠️
Note: For foreign suppliers receiving payments from a Datavant US entity, the applicable W-8BEN or W-8BEN-E is always required in addition to any local country-specific document.

📝 Module Two

Requesting a Contract

Submit in sequence (NDA, MSA, SOW, BAA or DPA) on Datavant paper, with standard commercial terms.

2.1 Contract Request Submission

Chronological sequence matters profoundly. Submit contract requests in Coupa using this specific structural blueprint:

Phase 1

NDA (Non-Disclosure Agreement)

Submit FIRST, before any other agreement ONLY if applicable i.e. we are sharing confidential information ahead of signing the broader agreement.

Phase 2

MSA (Master Service Agreement)

Governs the overall core legal framework and commercial relationship rules.

Phase 3

SOW (Statement of Work)

Defines exact contextual project scope, specific localized deliverables, and itemized execution timelines.

Phase 4

BAA / DPA (Data Protection Addendums)

BAA: Required when PHI is involved (either direct access or inadvertent risk).
DPA: Mandatory when PII metadata is touched across any system boundary.

Life Cycle

Amendments & Terminations

Amendment: Document modifications that require explicit contextual reference to the original executed agreement.
Termination: Formally ends the active contractual framework safely.

⚠️
Important: If a supplier has a separate MSA and a separate SOW, each agreement requires its own individual contract request in Coupa.

2.2 Contract Best Practices & Vendor Tiers

  • Sequence matters: Always submit an NDA first, followed by MSA → SOW → BAA / DPA as applicable, especially when PHI or PII is involved.
  • Use Datavant Paper: Wherever possible, use Datavant's standard contract templates rather than counterparty / supplier paper to reduce review loops and speed up turnaround time (TAT).
  • Disclose AI Usage: Be aware if the vendor is providing an artificial intelligence based service to Datavant and ensure that you disclose that on the Coupa ticket.

Be aware of the tier of your vendor before you put in the request:

Tier 0
Critical Infrastructure: Downtime directly impairs our capability to deliver core services to customers or causes immediate broad client impacts.
Tier 1
Data Processing Access: Direct system connectivity to, or active processing pipelines involving, customer PHI or PII.
Tier 2
Inadvertent Exposure Risk: No technical software guardrails prevent the vendor from accidentally viewing customer PHI or PII records.
Tier 3
Standard Confidential Track: Only accesses standard internal Datavant business information. Zero customer or personnel records exposed.
Tier 4
Insulated Perimeter: Absolutely no internal system data, network access, or tracking footprints.
⚠️
Please be aware if you are putting in a request for a vendor that falls into Tiers 0-2 there will be a stringent review from legal, compliance, and security. Plan ahead for making these requests as they may take more time to negotiate/process.

2.4 No PO, No Pay Policy

🚫
Datavant enforces a strict NO PO, NO PAY policy. Business owners and requestors must submit the Purchase Requisition in Coupa immediately after contract execution. The PO ensures the supplier can invoice correctly and enables timely payment, preventing last-minute escalations.

2.5 Contract Execution & DocuSign Protocol

All vendor contracts must be processed exclusively through Coupa. Datavant initiates DocuSign on all agreements after approval.

⚖️ Module Four

Approvals and the DOA

Approve by Total Contract Value, never self-approve, and escalate up rather than to a peer.

3.1 Overview & Core Principles

The Delegation of Authority defines the approval thresholds and authorized signatories for contract execution, and purchase requisition approvals. All approvals must be obtained prior to execution in Coupa.

  • No Self-Delegation: Authority cannot be self-delegated. A requestor cannot be their own approver at any threshold level.
  • No Verbal Sign-offs: Verbal approvals are not valid. All approvals must be documented within Coupa or via official written communication (email chain attached to the Coupa record).
  • Vertical Escalation Track: In the absence of the designated approver, authority escalates to the next level up. It does not pass laterally to a peer.
  • Governance Schedule: The DoA matrix is reviewed and updated annually by the Finance and Procurement teams. The most current version governs at all times.
⚠️
No individual may approve a transaction for which they are also the requestor. Self-approval is not permitted at any threshold level. Likewise, no individual may sign an agreement outside the authority set in this DOA.

3.2 Contract Approval Thresholds Through Coupa

Approval authority is based strictly on the Total Contract Value (TCV), inclusive of all years, amendments, and out-of-pocket expenses.

📄 Module Five

Datavant-Approved Templates

Always pull the latest template from Coupa. Supplier paper only with Legal and Procurement sign-off.

4.1 Overview & Use Case

Datavant maintains a library of pre-approved, legally vetted contract templates for all standard agreement types. These templates represent Datavant's preferred contractual positions and have been reviewed and approved by Legal, Compliance, Finance, and Procurement. Using these templates is the default requirement for all supplier engagements, not optional.

4.2 Why Approved Templates Matter

Mitigate risk vectors across internal ecosystems using structured base profiles:

⚖️ Legal & Compliance

Without Template: Supplier paper may contain unfavorable liability, indemnity, or IP clauses that expose Datavant.

With Approved Template: Pre-negotiated, legally sound positions protect Datavant's interests natively.

🔒 Data Privacy (PHI / PII)

Without Template: Non-standard DPA / BAA language may not meet HIPAA or applicable data privacy requirements.

With Approved Template: Compliant language perfectly aligned to Datavant's explicit regulatory obligations.

⏱️ Turnaround Time (TAT)

Without Template: Supplier paper requires full legal redline review, significantly increasing cycles.

With Approved Template: Datavant paper is pre-vetted, so Legal review is faster and focused only on commercial terms.

💰 Commercial Terms

Without Template: Supplier templates often default to supplier-friendly payment, auto-renewal, and liability terms.

With Approved Template: Datavant standard terms (NET 60, no auto-renew, no late fee) are pre-embedded.

⚙️ Coupa Processing

Without Template: Non-standard documents require additional reviews and delay supplier activation.

With Approved Template: Pre-approved templates flow through Coupa smoothly with fewer exception flags.

📊 Audit & Compliance

Without Template: Custom or ad-hoc agreements are significantly harder to track, audit, and enforce.

With Approved Template: Standardized templates make structural audits, renewals, and amendments straightforward.

⚠️
Always use the latest template version available in Coupa. Do not reuse a previously downloaded copy, since templates are updated periodically by Legal.

4.4 Policy Violations: What You Must Not Do

  • Do NOT execute or sign any agreement outside of the Coupa workflow.
  • Do NOT verbally agree to non-standard terms with a supplier and attempt to back-fill documentation afterward.
  • Do not allow a supplier to begin services or work until the contract is fully executed.
